Main system controller:

🔻 Core2 Stack controller recieves data from the weight sensors along with NFC sensor and according to weights data, guide the patient to perform correct pressure to achive better walking balance.
🔻 provide voice and visual guidence along with led lights that give the patient more comfort session.

Weights sensors system:

🔻 Weight sensors collect the pressure data from the patient session, so we could analyze the divergence and on which side.

🔻 the divergence data will help the physiotherapist to know the patients Rehabilitation progress without staying with them all the session.

🔻Max pressure capacity of each weight sensor is up to 20 kg.

Card reader:

🔻The smart card reader would be given to each patient.
🔻From the card reader the system can identify the patient who will start the session. 🔻The card reader enhance the security and make sure all the patients personal information will be private
🔻The sensors will not send any data untill card was inserted
🔻When patient put the card inside, the system will use speaker to welcome the patient, immediately after the session could be start.

Distance controller:

🔻The distance calculated by a formula that we developed a specially for that.

🔻Each round that the walker wheel does, is taking into a count and participating in the formula we have made.

🔻Mesure units in meters.

🔻Gives indication about the patient progress.

Gamification:

Missions:

🔻In the pursuit of enhancing user engagement and motivation, we will design a system of missions. These missions are dynamic tasks assigned to users, tied to specific distances achieved during their rehabilitation sessions. Upon reaching predefined milestones, a delightful smiley face appears on the user's interface, accompanied by a personalized message. This not only serves as a form of positive reinforcement but also creates a sense of accomplishment, encouraging users to consistently meet their goals.

Leaderboards:

🔻To foster a spirit of camaraderie and competition within the rehabilitation community, we will introduce the concept of leaderboards. Patients using walkers within the same department are connected to a friendly competition. At the end of each week or month, the patient who tops the leaderboard receives a small token of appreciation, such as a candy.

🔻This mechanism not only adds an element of fun and motivation but also encourages patients to push their boundaries and excel in their rehabilitation efforts. It promotes a sense of belonging to a supportive community and provides an extra incentive for users to strive for improvement.

Badges:

🔻Acknowledging and celebrating individual progress is a cornerstone of our project. We will implement a badge system to recognize and reward users for their achievements in real-time. Users receive notifications related to their walking speed, comparing it to their previous maximum speed. When a user surpasses their prior best, they are awarded a new badge.

🔻These badges act as a visual representation of their progress and accomplishments, motivating users to continuously challenge themselves and strive for higher levels of performance. It fosters a sense of personal achievement and pride in their rehabilitation journey, reinforcing the idea that every step forward is a noteworthy accomplishment.
